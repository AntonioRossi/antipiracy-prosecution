## 5. Plausible § 103 routes

1. **A20 + B9 + A4 or A6.** A20 supplies forensic individualization through EDL-generated versions, recipient registration, and lookup motivation; B9 supplies variable user-dependent switch points; A4/A6 supply manifests, recipient-specific sequences, suspect-pattern recovery, and recipient resolution. The missing relationship is plural modification of paired out/in-point entries while retaining identified physical-camera order, followed by reconstruction of the delivered manifest from plural detected cut time codes.
2. **A20 + direct multicamera/EDL art + A13.** A20 supplies individualized EDLs and enforcement motivation; direct production art may supply physical-camera entries and boundary editing; A13 supplies manifest regeneration. The critical questions are why a skilled artisan would choose plural physical-camera cut boundaries as the recipient code, build the delivered manifest from those choices, and reconstruct it from scene-change-derived cut times with a reasonable expectation of success.
3. **Conventional multicamera/EDL practice + B6 + A6.** B6 supplies local timing variation and robust reacquisition; A6 supplies recipient temporal sequences and lookup. The claimed paired entry modification and exact manifest-reconstruction relationship remain to be supplied.
4. **A4 + B9 + B7/A5.** A4 supplies perspective alternatives, manifests, recovery, and user resolution; B9 supplies variable actual switch points; B7/A5 supply cut comparison. This route is stronger against the suspect-side operations than against the structured physical-camera production operation.

Do not characterize A20 as teaching away. Its unique EDL per consumer and product-signature language supports a forensic-individualization motivation. KSR rationales based on known-element combination, applying a known technique, or predictable variation are facially plausible. The response must focus on the exact relationships and evidentiary gaps, not the number of references. Under [MPEP § 2143](https://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/pac/mpep/s2143.html), every claimed limitation and relationship must be accounted for. Under [MPEP § 2144.03](https://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/pac/mpep/s2144.html), common knowledge or official notice should not replace evidence for the central disputed relationship after an adequate specific traverse.
