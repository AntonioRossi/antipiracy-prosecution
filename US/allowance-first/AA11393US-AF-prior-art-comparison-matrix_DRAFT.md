## 5. Plausible § 103 routes

1. **A20 + B9 + A4 or A6.** A20 supplies forensic individualization through EDL-generated versions, recipient registration, and lookup motivation; B9 supplies variable user-dependent switch points; A4/A6 supply manifests, recipient-specific sequences, suspect-pattern recovery, and recipient resolution. The missing relationship is plural modification of paired out/in-point entries while retaining identified physical-camera order, followed by reconstruction of the delivered manifest from plural detected cut time codes.
2. **A20 + direct multicamera/EDL art + A13/A21.** A20 supplies individualized EDLs and enforcement motivation; direct production art may supply physical-camera entries and boundary editing; A13 supplies manifest regeneration; A21 supplies shot-boundary-derived duration-sequence fingerprinting and exact database equality. The critical questions are why a skilled artisan would choose plural physical-camera cut boundaries as the recipient code, build the delivered manifest from those choices, and reconstruct the delivered manifest rather than a generic video fingerprint from scene-change-derived cut times with a reasonable expectation of success.
3. **Conventional multicamera/EDL practice + B6 + A6.** B6 supplies local timing variation and robust reacquisition; A6 supplies recipient temporal sequences and lookup. The claimed paired entry modification and exact manifest-reconstruction relationship remain to be supplied.
4. **A4 + B9 + A21 or B7/A5.** A4 supplies perspective alternatives, manifests, recovery, and user resolution; B9 supplies variable actual switch points; A21 supplies shot-boundary duration-sequence fingerprinting and exact database equality, while B7/A5 supply other cut-comparison techniques. This route is stronger against the suspect-side operations than against the structured physical-camera production operation.
5. **AF claim 23 — A4/A6 + A13 + A21, with A20 or B9.** A4/A6 supply recipient-specific manifests, temporal patterns, recovery, and user resolution; A13 supplies manifest regeneration; A21 supplies shot-boundary timing sequences and exact database equality; and A20 or B9 supplies personalization or switch-timing motivation. The remaining question is why a skilled artisan would reconstruct a delivered reference/mate manifest carrying a mate-containing recipient timing combination and use the same detected plural combination for lookup, rather than compare a generic duration fingerprint. AF claim 23 cannot rely on paired EDL modifications or retained physical-camera source order to answer this route.

Do not characterize A20 as teaching away. Its unique EDL per consumer and product-signature language supports a forensic-individualization motivation. KSR rationales based on known-element combination, applying a known technique, or predictable variation are facially plausible. The response must focus on the exact relationships and evidentiary gaps, not the number of references. Under [MPEP § 2143](https://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/pac/mpep/s2143.html), every claimed limitation and relationship must be accounted for. Under [MPEP § 2144.03](https://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/pac/mpep/s2144.html), common knowledge or official notice should not replace evidence for the central disputed relationship after an adequate specific traverse.

## 8. Bottom line and search priority

No reviewed document was identified disclosing AF claims 1, 19, or 23 as a whole. For AF claims 1 and 19, the strongest current distinction is the operational conjunction of plural paired time-code modifications in a structured list while retaining physical-camera identifiers and order, manifest-based recipient distribution, reconstruction from plural suspect cut time codes, and equal-manifest ledger lookup. AF claim 23 omits those production operations and is carried instead by the delivered reference/mate manifest environment and the mate-containing delivered-combination-to-reconstructed-same-combination nexus.

The operative reconstructed-manifest formulations face credible combination pressure from A20+B9+A4/A6/A13/A21, and direct multicamera personalization or vision-mixer/EDL art could materially strengthen the integrated-claim route. A4/A6+A13+A21 already pressure almost the complete recovery chain of AF claim 23. A21 also narrows any technical-effect argument based solely on robust shot-boundary recovery, including cropping/rotation robustness. DW-08A therefore remains the most important unresolved search: personalized versions created by moving physical-camera selection boundaries, temporally aligned alternate-angle screeners, switch-boundary fingerprints, scene-change-derived delivered-manifest reconstruction, and monitoring products that allocate detection, reconstruction, and the search-to-identify operation across separate systems or actors.
