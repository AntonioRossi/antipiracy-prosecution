## 3. Required sequence

### A. Assign and initiate now

1. Name the US attorney responsible for DW-01 through DW-10.
2. Name the EP attorney responsible for DW-07, DW-11A, and DW-11B.
3. Ask PRAXI/Italian counsel for the status and expected delivery of the Italian search report.
4. Start the B6 official-register check, B8 file-history review, and retrieval of A2/A3/A5/B7/Tardos.
5. Collect the applicant's corporate, assignment, employment, licensing, and entity-status facts.
6. Docket the US, PCT informal-comment, Italian-monitoring, and EP deadlines with a second-person verification, including the nominal and apparently weekend-adjusted European-phase entry dates and an earlier internal deadline.

### B. Complete before the US filing package is approved

1. Record the §371/bypass and Track One decision.
2. Approve the final claim set and confirm that any Track One set stays within 30 total claims, four independent claims, and no multiple-dependent claim.
3. Complete the claim-by-claim provisional/PCT support review, including the Example 2 issue and claim 16 terminology.
4. Finalize ADS, continuity/benefit information, oath/declaration handling, applicant identity, assignment chain, entity status, and priority-document handling.
5. Complete the initial IDS materiality decision and obtain every copy, translation, abstract, or explanation counsel determines is required at filing.
6. Confirm that the specification and drawings are the intended as-filed documents and contain no unacknowledged new matter.

### C. Continue after filing

1. Confirm every filing receipt, benefit claim, applicant entry, claim count, fee payment, and Track One disposition.
2. Monitor Italian, EP, PCT, and related US matters for new citations or inconsistent positions.
3. File supplemental IDS papers within the applicable window when counsel determines that new material information must be submitted.
4. Preserve examiner initials/consideration records and all correspondence showing how submitted information was handled.

### D. Complete European-phase entry

1. Have EP counsel independently confirm the 31-month entry deadline, the application of Rule 134(1), and an earlier internal filing date.
2. Complete the applicable Rule 159 entry acts, including the translation if required, identification of the application documents for the European grant procedure, and the applicable filing, designation, search, examination, and renewal-fee actions.
3. Select the EP claim set, calculate the Rule 162 fees for the sixteenth and each later claim, and document the entry-stage payment and amendment plan.
4. Preserve the entry papers, payment evidence, filing receipt, and an independent post-filing record check.

### E. Complete the Rule 161/162 response after communication

1. Save the actual EPO communication and docket its six-month period with second-person verification.
2. Address the Written Opinion and any invited corrections, and file any approved amendments to the description, claims, or drawings.
3. Recalculate and pay any claims fees due under Rule 162 on the basis of the claims filed within the Rule 161 period, and record the treatment of every claim for which a fee is not paid.
4. Complete the cross-jurisdiction consistency review and preserve the filed response, payment evidence, and receipt.

## 4. Intervening-information escalation

Immediately refer the matter to US counsel if a reference, activity, or other potentially material information is identified with an effective prior-art date after 26 February 2024 but before 19 February 2025, or if any Office questions entitlement to the provisional date.

Before making a substantive response, counsel should reassess:

- claim-by-claim entitlement to the provisional date;
- the provisional Example 2 inconsistency;
- whether the effective filing date is outcome-determinative;
- US disclosure obligations and timing;
- consistency with PCT, EP, Italian, and other counterpart positions; and
- every statement asserting or implying entitlement to the earlier date.

This escalation is not limited to documents carrying a publication date in the interval. It includes later-published patent documents with a potentially relevant effective prior-art date and non-documentary information such as alleged public use, sale, or offer-for-sale activity.

Do not use the log to make an applicant admission that an item is prior art. It documents handling and counsel's disclosure decision.

## 6. EP entry and Rule 161/162 handoff contents

### A. Rule 159 entry file

The EP entry file should include:

1. EP counsel's written confirmation of the nominal 26 September 2026 date, the apparent Rule 134(1) extension to 28 September 2026, and the earlier internal deadline;
2. the entry form and applicant, representative, priority, and bibliographic data;
3. the selected application documents and any required translation;
4. an applicability and payment checklist for the Rule 159 entry acts and fees; and
5. the selected EP claim set, Rule 162 claims-fee calculation and strategy, filing receipt, and independent record check.

### B. Rule 161/162 response file

The EP handoff should include:

1. the actual Rule 161/162 communication and independently verified six-month deadline;
2. the PCT application and Written Opinion;
3. the final or proposed WIPO informal comments;
4. the actor-focused US claim set, clearly marked as a US strategy rather than an EP amendment instruction;
5. the D1 issue-by-issue correction and supplemental prior-art matrix;
6. the provisional/PCT support map and confidential Example 2 analysis;
7. all Italian search results and newly identified references;
8. a cross-jurisdiction terminology table;
9. a written reconciliation of any US and EP claim or argument differences; and
10. the Rule 162 claims-fee calculation for the claims maintained or amended during the Rule 161 period.

EP counsel should independently select EPC-compliant claims and arguments. The US package should guide consistency, not substitute for an EPC added-matter, clarity, unity, and inventive-step analysis.

Operative rule texts: [EPC Rule 159](https://www.epo.org/en/legal/epc/2020/r159.html), [EPC Rule 134](https://www.epo.org/en/legal/epc/2020/r134.html), [EPC Rule 161](https://www.epo.org/en/legal/epc/2020/r161.html), and [EPC Rule 162](https://www.epo.org/en/legal/epc/2020/r162.html). These links and internal dates do not replace EP counsel's current-law review and controlled docket.

## 7. Escalation conditions

Escalate immediately to the responsible attorney if:

- a filing or response deadline cannot be independently confirmed;
- an inventor will not or cannot sign;
- the applicant/assignee chain contains a gap or conflicting owner;
- entity status is uncertain;
- a retrieved reference maps the alternate-camera boundary mechanism more closely than the present matrix;
- the Italian or EPO search identifies new material art;
- any proposed amendment exceeds the Track One numerical limits;
- any claim wording lacks clear provisional or PCT support; or
- a US, EP, Italian, PCT, or other position becomes inconsistent with another proceeding.

## 8. Completion standard

This deferred-work program is complete only when:

1. every register item has a named owner and docketed gate;
2. all required documents and official records are stored or linked in the repository or counsel's controlled file;
3. counsel's route, priority, disclosure, formalities, ownership, entity-status, and EP decisions are recorded in writing;
4. every required filing has a receipt and independent record check; and
5. continuing monitoring remains assigned through issuance, abandonment, or formal transfer of responsibility.

An unchecked reminder, an email request without follow-up, or the mere presence of a citation in this repository does not satisfy the completion standard.
