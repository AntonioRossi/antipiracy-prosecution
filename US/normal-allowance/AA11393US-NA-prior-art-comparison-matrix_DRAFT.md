## 1. Proposed combination to test

The proposed inventive combination is:

1. video from multiple cameras capturing temporally corresponding views;
2. a structured edit list recording source-camera selections and cut time codes;
3. local movement of a camera-selection boundary so that the same ordered first-camera-to-second-camera transition occurs at noncoincident timings in a reference and a mate, with temporally corresponding frames from different cameras in the intervening interval;
4. optionally, preservation or restoration of a later boundary;
5. association of the resulting camera-cut timing pattern or manifest representation with a recipient; and
6. derivation and matching, from suspected content, of a camera-source-transition pattern that includes corresponding switch timings to resolve the recipient.

Terminology in this matrix follows the claim strategy: **camera-cut timing pattern** denotes the timing representation used by the distribution and end-to-end families and, in NA claims 9 and 22, is constrained by the identified source pair and preserved switch timing; **camera-source-transition pattern** denotes the detection-side organization of ordered source transitions and corresponding switch timings at candidate-distinguishing regions for operational comparison. NA claim 1 recites the corresponding ordered-transition and noncoincident-timing output relationship directly rather than through a pattern term. The terms perform different claim functions rather than defining a strict information-content hierarchy, and neither is merely a label applied to generic timing data.

The six-part combination above is the portfolio target, not a statement that every actor-split independent claim recites all six parts. NA claims 1, 9, and 16 isolate different actors; NA claim 22 carries the closed loop as the patentability anchor. Sections 4–6 assess the language actually carried by each NA claim and do not treat an intended “camera-boundary origin” as a limitation unless the claim recites resulting structure or an operation that produces it.

### Combination theories counsel should be prepared to distinguish

1. **A4 + B9:** A4 supplies camera-perspective alternatives, customized manifests, unauthorized-copy version recovery, database comparison, and recipient resolution; B9 supplies shot-cut-informed, key-dependent actual switch positions and stream-of-origin codes. The response is not that A4 has “no timing” or B9 has a fixed grid. It is that neither teaches using synchronized different-camera feeds to move a recorded `A→B` edit boundary while retaining the same ordered source transition, and counsel should test whether the combination would predictably yield that structure rather than merely alternative perspectives or transformed-copy switches.
2. **A6 + A5/B7:** A6 supplies recipient-specific temporal-event generation, manifests, detection, comparison, and source resolution; A5/B7 supply known shot/cut detection. The response must be the ordered camera-source pair and moved EDL boundary at a candidate-distinguishing region, not generic temporal-pattern matching.
3. **Conventional EDL/multicamera practice + B9 or B6:** this is the principal unresolved search/obviousness route against NA claim 1. B9/B6 provide reasons to individualize switch positions or local widths for collusion resistance.
