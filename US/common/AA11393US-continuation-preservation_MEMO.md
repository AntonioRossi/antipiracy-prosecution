> **SHARED US CONTINUATION CONTROL · STATUS 23 JULY 2026**
>
> **INTERNAL EXECUTION CONTROL — NOT A FILED PAPER OR LEGAL OPINION.** The responsible US attorney must confirm current law, the actual benefit chain, copendency, inventorship, support, deadlines, and filing evidence.
>
> **CONTINUATION STATUS: NOT YET PRESERVED.** The controlled-continuation procedure applies whether the ordinary § 371 parent uses `NA`, `AF`, or an approved hybrid. No successor filing, filing receipt, or recognized successor benefit relationship is recorded.

## 1. Current architecture

The current US filing direction is ordinary, non-prioritized national-stage entry under 35 U.S.C. § 371 for PCT/IB2025/051755. The same ordinary examination posture applies to each controlled successor.

The intended benefit chain is:

> US provisional 63/557,868, filed 26 February 2024 → PCT/IB2025/051755, filed 19 February 2025 → ordinary § 371 US national-stage parent → ordinary § 111(a) continuation or a divisional when supported by the restriction record.

The PCT priority claim and the US national-stage claim to benefit under 35 U.S.C. § 119(e) require official-record verification. Each later application requires counsel-approved relationship wording, a qualifying benefit chain, and copendency. The exact parent claims remain subject to selection of `NA`, `AF`, or an approved hybrid under [`../README.md`](../README.md).

`AF-CONT-2026-07-22-v2` is one candidate successor claim set, not the universal continuation procedure. The selected parent topology controls which supported claims remain reserved.

## 2. Pendency rule

For each generation in which supported, commercially valuable scope remains deliberately deferred, file and verify an approved successor while a qualifying application remains pending, or record an applicant-approved chain-closure decision before that application issues or otherwise loses pendency.

A notice of allowance is the ordinary execution trigger. The internal target is filing and verification before or contemporaneously with issue-fee payment. Any threatened abandonment, termination, or other loss of pendency accelerates the trigger. An RCE, continuation, divisional, or continuation-in-part is not interchangeable with another vehicle; counsel must select the vehicle supported by the live record.

This control is recursive only while supported, commercially valuable scope remains deferred. A verified successor becomes the next controlled parent when counsel confirms that it qualifies. Otherwise the generation ends with the chain-closure outcome in § 7.

## 5. Claim, support, and disclosure firewall

Before a reserved claim is approved, counsel must record its exact provisional, PCT, and parent support; separate written-description and enablement conclusions; DW-05A Mode A, B, or C; intervening-art consequence; actor and proof posture; and current art assessment. A continuation preserves qualifying pendency only. It does not create disclosure, cure new matter, broaden the provisional, or establish entitlement to an earlier date.

Strategy-specific claim text and analysis remain in the versioned `NA`, `AF`, and `AF-CONT` claim sets, support maps, matrices, and crosswalk. This memo does not transfer a support, art, eligibility, infringement, or priority conclusion between strategies.

The sole disclosure inventory is [`AA11393US-US_IDS-reference-list_DRAFT.md`](AA11393US-US_IDS-reference-list_DRAFT.md). Counsel must decide IDS treatment separately for the § 371 parent and every § 111(a) successor; appearance in the PCT, parent, repository, or another family member does not itself establish consideration in the successor. Public PCT positions remain controlled by [`AA11393US-PCT_informal-comments-IB_DRAFT.md`](AA11393US-PCT_informal-comments-IB_DRAFT.md).

Restriction, divisional, § 121 consonance, double patenting, terminal disclaimers, ownership, transactions, shared term, and remaining commercial life must be assessed on the actual parent and successor claims. Reissue is not a controlled substitute for preserving copendency.

## 6. Event workflow

### At § 371 parent filing

1. Record the selected `NA`, `AF`, or hybrid topology and every deferred family under CONT-03.
2. Complete CONT-01 and CONT-02 using the filed parent record.
3. Verify the § 119(e) benefit claim, applicant data, filed claims, fees, and shared IDS treatment.

### During parent prosecution

1. Update CONT-03 after any amendment, cancellation, restriction/election, new art, support decision, or material business change.
2. Preserve consistent support, priority, art, inventorship, ownership, and IDS positions across every related case.
3. Complete CONT-04 before the ordinary execution trigger or any earlier threat to pendency.

### At and after successor filing

1. Complete CONT-05 while qualifying pendency exists.
2. Review the filing receipt and recognized benefit relationship immediately.
3. Complete CONT-06 for the filed generation.
4. Apply CONT-01 through CONT-06 to a next controlled generation only if supported, commercially valuable scope remains deferred.

## 7. Outcome labels

### `CONTINUATION PRESERVED`

Use only when a successor was filed during qualifying pendency; the submitted papers, fees, and acknowledgment are preserved; the filing receipt and benefit relationship were reviewed; the selected claims have current support and art records; IDS responsibility is assigned; and preserved, omitted, and further-deferred scope is identified.

### `CHAIN CLOSED — DEFERRED SCOPE NOT PRESERVED`

Use only when the applicant and lead US counsel approve no further successor before loss of qualifying pendency and the current record identifies the controlled parent, every relinquished reservation, and the resulting coverage disposition.

Until one outcome is fully evidenced, the operative status is **NOT YET PRESERVED**.

## 8. Escalation conditions

Escalate immediately if the parent may lose pendency; a notice of allowance issues without CONT-04 completion; the issue-fee target approaches while CONT-05 is incomplete; a filing receipt omits or misstates a benefit relationship; desired claims appear to require new matter; inventorship or ownership differs; a restriction/divisional or § 121 position is uncertain; a terminal disclaimer or ownership transaction is proposed; or supported, commercially valuable scope remains deferred without a current parent, owner, docket, successor decision, or chain-closure disposition.
